What Makes a Good Portfolio?

From in-class lectures and browsing other people's portfolios, I have picked up on a few trends. The most important one is that you want to actually display your work. It is not enough to say that you did something. If you created a website, for example, you should provide it so others can see what you can do.

You also want to choose projects relevant to what you are applying for. If it's a design internship, put design projects. If it's a more code heavy internship, then lean that way. In other words, don't just include every random thing you've done. And its useful to explain how you created the project or what you learned, not just what the final product looks like.

I suppose the other thing is to keep it relevant and just think about what the company/program is actually looking for. You don't have to put everything you've ever made in here, just what is appropriate to what you are going for.
